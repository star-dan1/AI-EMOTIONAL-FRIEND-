  INTRODUCTION 
EMOTIONAL ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE     
  Emotional Artificial Intelligence refers to a branch of Artificial Intelligence that can recognize, interpret, and respond to human emotions. It combines the understanding of human feelings with machine learning, natural language processing, voice analysis, and other technologies.
  Emotional Artificial Intelligence is about making machines more human-aware. Instead of answering only logically or objectively, it can sense how you feel, whether you‚Äôre sad, happy, angry, or stressed, and respond in a way that shows empathy and care.
  The goal is not just to understand emotions, but also to respond appropriately by providing comfort, encouragement, and guidance. What we envision is an AI companion capable of offering empathetic conversations in English, Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo.

Building an Emotion Detection MLP for Yoruba, Igbo, and Hausa
Building an MLP for emotion detection in these languages is a major challenge due to the scarcity of high-quality labeled data. However, the tools provide a clear path forward.
1. Data and Feature Generation (Pre-MLP)
The biggest barrier is data scarcity. Assuming some labeled text data is available, you must convert the text into numerical features:
1.Preprocessing (NLTK/spaCy/Python): Clean the text data by removing noise, punctuation, and non-language characters. Due to the unique nature of these languages, this step often requires custom rules implemented in Python.
2.Word Embedding (Gensim): Train a FastText model on a large, unlabeled corpus of text from the target language (e.g., Igbo). 3.Sentence Vector: The word vectors for all words in a sentence are aggregated (e.g., averaged) to create a single, fixed-size input vector for that sentence.

A simple MLP using averaged embeddings is often outperformed by modern Transformer models.

2.Fine-Tuning: Take the AfriBERTa model and fine-tune it on your relatively smaller, emotion-labeled dataset. This is a far more robust and efficient way to achieve high-accuracy emotion detection, as the model already possesses a strong understanding of the language's grammar and semantics from its massive initial pre-training.
